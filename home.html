Penguin Books

Originally published in the United Kingdom, Penguin Books was concieved of by Sir Allen Lane in 1935 as a more literary alternative to the paperbacks that were sold to the general public at that time. Penguin paperbacks were sold for sixpence in Woolworth's and other stores, as well as in train stations through vending machines called Penguinators. Penguin's immediate success was evidence that well written, good quality paperback books marketed to the general public was a commercially viable scheme. It was an idea that radically changed the book publishing industry.

The Penguin Collector

The Penguin Collector, the journal of the Penguin Collectors Society. The Penguin Collectors Society publishes The Penguin Collector twice a year. The Society was founded in 1974 by a group of enthusiasts meeting in Richmond, Surrey.
