The Limited Editions Club

The Limited Editions Club was started in 1929 by George Macy. Members paid a subscription for the classic titles, which were illustrated by contemporary artists. They were published in relatively small quantities, and are now highly prized by collectors.

Art and Literature

Some of the more well-known artists who illustrated these books include Henri Matisse (who illustrated Ulysses, by James Joyce), Pablo Picasso, Norman Rockwell and Edward Steichen.
