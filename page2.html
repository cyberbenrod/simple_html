Everyman's Library

Everyman's Library was first published as a series in 1906 by Joseph Malby Dent. The books were pocket-sized and the endpapers beautifully designed in the style of William Morris. A London publisher, Dent envisioned a 1,000-volume library for all readers. The originally price was only a shilling. Their low cost and good quality gave them a democratic appeal. Five hundred volumes were published by 1910, and the thousandth volume was published in 1956.

William Morris Style

The high quality of these books was evidenced by their beautiful endpapers, designed to emulate the work of William Morris
